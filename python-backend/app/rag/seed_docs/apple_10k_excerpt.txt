APPLE INC. — ANNUAL REPORT (10-K) EXCERPT
Fiscal Year Ended September 30, 2024

BUSINESS OVERVIEW

Apple Inc. designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories, and sells a variety of related services. The Company's fiscal year is the 52- or 53-week period that ends on the last Saturday of September.

FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS — FISCAL 2024

Net sales: $391.0 billion (FY2024) vs $383.3 billion (FY2023), a 2.0% year-over-year increase.
Net income: $93.7 billion (FY2024) vs $97.0 billion (FY2023).
Earnings per diluted share (EPS): $6.11 (FY2024) vs $6.13 (FY2023).
Gross margin: 46.2% (FY2024) vs 44.1% (FY2023) — highest in over a decade.
Operating income: $123.2 billion (FY2024).
Free cash flow: $108.8 billion (FY2024).
Cash and marketable securities: $153.0 billion (FY2024).
Total debt: $97.3 billion (FY2024).

RISKS AND CHALLENGES

1. iPhone Concentration Risk: iPhone accounts for over 51% of revenue. Any slowdown in smartphone upgrade cycles, loss of carrier subsidies, or competitive pressure (particularly from Huawei and Samsung in China) could materially impact revenue.

2. Greater China Exposure: China represents 17% of revenue. US-China trade tensions, data localisation requirements, and nationalist consumer sentiment toward domestic brands (Huawei) represent ongoing risks.

3. Regulatory Risk — App Store: The European Union's Digital Markets Act (DMA) now requires Apple to allow third-party app stores on iOS in EU member states, threatening App Store commission revenue (~$30B+ annually). Similar regulations may be adopted in the US and other jurisdictions.

4. Services Growth Dependence: Apple's valuation premium is substantially attributed to Services growth. Any deceleration in App Store revenue, iCloud subscriptions, or Apple Pay transaction volumes would likely compress the P/E multiple.

5. AI Integration: Apple's on-device AI initiative "Apple Intelligence" (announced WWDC 2024) requires iPhone 15 Pro or later hardware, potentially driving an upgrade supercycle in FY2025. However, Apple has disclosed limited monetisation details.

MANAGEMENT DISCUSSION — REVENUE GROWTH DRIVERS

CEO Tim Cook highlighted Services as the primary long-term growth driver: "Services revenue achieved a new all-time record in every geographic segment in Q4 2024. With 1 billion paid subscriptions across our services portfolio, we are well-positioned to continue growing this high-margin revenue stream."

Apple's installed base of active devices exceeded 2.2 billion globally as of September 2024, providing a large addressable market for services monetisation.

RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT

R&D spending: $31.4 billion (FY2024) vs $29.9 billion (FY2023).
R&D as % of revenue: 8.0%.
Primary focus areas: Apple Intelligence (generative AI), Vision Pro spatial computing platform, silicon design (M4 chip), and health sensors (Apple Watch Series 10).

SUPPLY CHAIN & MANUFACTURING

Apple has diversified manufacturing away from China toward India (iPhone 15/16 production) and Vietnam (AirPods, Apple Watch). The company aims to source 25% of iPhone production from India by 2025 to reduce geopolitical concentration risk.

CONCLUSION — INVESTMENT CONSIDERATIONS

Apple is a world-class capital allocator with durable competitive moats: the iOS ecosystem, App Store network effects, supply chain excellence, and premium brand positioning. The Services segment, growing at 14% annually and carrying 74% gross margins, is the primary valuation driver. Key risks are Greater China exposure, App Store regulatory headwinds, and iPhone replacement cycle elongation. The stock trades at a premium multiple relative to the S&P 500, requiring sustained double-digit Services growth to justify current valuation.
